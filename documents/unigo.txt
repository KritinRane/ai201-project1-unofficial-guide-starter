Year: Current Student | Date: Dec 2025
Being part of a small school is an introvert's dream. Stevens is unique in that despite a smaller student population, there is a plethora of opportunities for every person. Professors have always been highly supportive and always willing to help, as long as you are willing to ask for it.

FAQ: What should every freshman know before they start?
That a lot of your friends will forget about you once you go to college, don't hang on too tightly.

---
FAQ: Describe how your school looks to someone who's never seen it.
A small close knit community that you may get tired of after a while.

---
FAQ: Describe the students at your school.
They are helpful and easy to get along with.

---
FAQ: What's the one thing you wish someone had told you about freshman year?
The food actually sucks. Stevens tries to wine and dine you when pre-frosh visit, but then bring out the crappy food on a daily basis.

---
FAQ: What do you consider the worst thing about your school?
The dorms have problems with bed bugs and mice. They are not very clean and always need repair. The food in the cafeteria is not great. There are not many options for students who are vegetarians or have special needs. It costs a lot of money and for what we pay there are not many things given back to students. The desks in classrooms are dirty and broken. The lockers in the buildings are broken and have not been fixed.

---
FAQ: Describe your favorite campus traditions.
Stevens is best known for its reputation as an engineering school. Alumni connections are very strong when seeking jobs, particularly in New Jersey. Several athletics teams have ranked in the top 20 in national Division III competition. The music and technology program has expanded rapidly with a variety of music related activities for students.

---
FAQ: What's the most frustrating thing about your school?
I'm interested in many areas of study but so far I have had to focus on core courses and have been unable to take courses in other programs.

---
FAQ: What is your overall opinion of this school?
Stevens campus life is rarely boring either because you're doing work or are partying in Hoboken or NYC. The work is hard, very hard, don't expect to be able to half-ass your way through it.

---
FAQ: What is the stereotype of students at your school?
As with any tech school, people mainly think everyone is a complete nerd. This is false. There are lots of different types of people at Stevens. Sure there are people who like LAN parties or the Anime Convention, but there are also people who play sports, party, and are super involved on campus. To go to a tech school you have to be a little bit of a nerd at heart though.

---
FAQ: What are the academics like?
Good grades, social life, enough sleep - pick two. But if you're good at time management you can get all three. Classes are easily bearable if you know when to focus and when to relax.

---
FAQ: What do you brag about most when you tell friends about your school?
The research opportunities at Stevens are amazing. Undergraduates can work with professors and graduate students over the summer through the Scholars program or Technogenesis. Students can live on campus while working in labs and are paid by the hour.
