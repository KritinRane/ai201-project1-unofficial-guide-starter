Degree: Music | Date: Mar 2026
I enjoyed my time at Stevens. Most professors in the M&T and humanities departments were wonderful. Others seemed uninterested in modern music production and business. Services offered by the career center ceased entirely a year after graduation. Resources for internships and job placement were non-existent for humanities students. I worry about future classes as professors continue to be replaced by individuals with increasingly specific and outdated fields of expertise.

Degree: Engineering Management | Date: May 2023
Stevens is led by administrators who do a great job advertising and then continually fail to deliver. Each round of tuition increases, prices rise while Stevens trims vital services like funding for clubs, career services, and greek organizations. What remains is a soulless institution recruiting at an unsustainable pace until students are unable to find housing, dealing with lazy and arrogant professors. The only thing this university truly has going for it is its location. If I could go back in time I would go anywhere else. This place truly broke my spirit.

Degree: Biomedical Engineering | Date: Oct 2019
Stevens has major fundamental problems with administrative backing. It takes days and multiple emails to get a response, and half the time responses are unhelpful. They charge a premium price for average education. Almost all techniques taught in the undergrad biology lab are outdated and obsolete. Stevens charges outrageous fees that do not benefit students. Graduate student fees are very high and programs are not convenient for working schedules. They also charge a $260 graduation fee and hold your finished degree hostage until you pay.

The curriculum and faculty helped me constantly think about finding solutions to issues my company faces, which has been noted by upper management.
